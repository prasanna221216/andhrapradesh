Places in Araku Valley

Borra Caves

  1. Borra Caves are among the largest and deepest limestone caves in India, located in the Ananthagiri Hills of the Eastern Ghats at an elevation of about 705 meters. These karstic caves extend up to 80 meters deep and span over approximately two square kilometers. They feature spectacular stalactite and stalagmite formations created over millions of years by the erosional action of water on limestone, resulting in unique shapes resembling animals, human figures, and deities, such as the famous natural Shivalinga inside the caves
  2. Discovered in 1807 by British geologist William King, Borra Caves hold immense archaeological importance. Paleolithic and Neolithic tools found in the region indicate early human habitation dating back tens of thousands of years. The caves also contain ancient inscriptions and have been a site of continuous natural formation, making them a valuable resource for geological and anthropological research.
  3. The caves are not only a natural marvel but also a significant cultural and religious site, housing a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, revered by local tribal communities. They attract numerous tourists and pilgrims, especially between October and March when the weather is pleasant. The caves contribute substantially to the local economy through tourism, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, spiritual significance, and historical heritage.
  1. Katiki Waterfalls is a stunning cascade located near Araku Valley, about 7 km from the famous Borra Caves in Visakhapatnam district, Andhra Pradesh. The waterfall is formed by the Gosthani River and falls from a height of approximately 50 to 100 feet, surrounded by dense forests and rocky terrain of the Eastern Ghats. This makes it an ideal spot for nature lovers, photographers, and those seeking tranquility amidst lush greenery.
  2. Reaching Katiki Falls requires a moderate trek of about 20-30 minutes through forested and hilly terrain, starting near the Borra Caves area. The trek offers a mix of easy and challenging paths, including rocky patches and streams, providing an adventurous experience along with opportunities to observe diverse flora and fauna. Proper footwear is recommended due to slippery and uneven surfaces, especially near the waterfall.
  3. Katiki Falls is also popular for camping, where visitors can enjoy bonfires, cook local delicacies, and spend the night under the stars. A unique local specialty is "Bamboo chicken," marinated chicken cooked inside bamboo sticks, which is highly sought after by tourists. Visitors are advised to avoid weekends and public holidays to escape crowds, be cautious of slippery paths, and prefer jeep transport as roads are rough for regular vehicles. The best time to visit is during the monsoon and post-monsoon months (August to December) when the waterfall is at its fullest and most beautiful.

Chaparai Waterfalls

  1. Chaparai Waterfalls, also known as Dumbriguda Waterfalls, is located about 13-15 km from Araku Valley in Andhra Pradesh. It is nestled amidst lush green forests and surrounded by dense vegetation, making it a picturesque and tranquil spot ideal for nature lovers. The waterfall is not a traditional vertical fall but a cascading stream flowing over smooth, slanting rock formations, creating natural water slides and pools perfect for swimming and relaxing
  2. The site offers exciting opportunities for trekking through the forest trails leading to the waterfall, with a moderately challenging trek of around 2 km. The natural water slides formed by polished rocks provide a thrilling experience for adventure seekers. Visitors can also enjoy camping, swimming, and picnicking in the serene environment. However, caution is advised during the monsoon season due to strong water flow and slippery rocks.
  3. The ideal visiting period is post-monsoon (October to February) when the water flow is pleasant and the surroundings are lush green. Although the waterfall can be visited year-round, the monsoon months (June to September) offer a spectacular view of the falls in full force but require extra caution. Entry is free, and the site is open from early morning to evening. Visitors are encouraged to respect the environment by avoiding littering and plastic use.

Visakapatnam

Places in Vishakapatnam

  1. The INS Kurusura Submarine Museum is housed inside a real, decommissioned Indian Navy submarine, INS Kurusura, which was a Russian-built I-641 class submarine commissioned in 1970 and decommissioned in 2001 after 31 years of service. It is the first submarine converted into a museum in Asia and the second in the world, making it a rare and significant naval heritage site.
  2. The museum offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the six compartments of the submarine, including the captainâ€™s cabin, crew sleeping quarters, kitchen, control room, radar and sonar rooms, and weapons storage. Human-sized models depict how submariners lived and worked in cramped, challenging conditions, providing a realistic insight into the hardships and daily life of naval officers underwater. Guides, many of whom are former Navy personnel, enhance the experience with detailed explanations.
  3. Located on Ramakrishna Beach in Visakhapatnam, the museum is well-maintained and attracts numerous tourists, history buffs, and military enthusiasts. It serves both as an educational resource on submarine technology and naval history and as a source of inspiration for youth interested in joining the Indian Navy. The museum also displays artifacts, photographs, and written scripts chronicling the evolution of submarines, contributing to public awareness about maritime defense.

Kailasagiri

  1. Kailasagiri is a prominent hilltop park in Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, situated at an altitude of about 173 meters. It offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Bay of Bengal, the Eastern Ghats, the city, beaches, and lush greenery. The park spans approximately 350-380 acres, making it a vast green space ideal for nature lovers and tourists seeking scenic beauty
  2. The park is famous for its 40-foot tall Shiva-Parvati statues, which symbolize divine presence and are a major draw for visitors. Other key attractions include a large floral clock (one of the largest in India with a 10-meter diameter), the Titanic Viewpoint offering spectacular sea views, the Shanku-Chakra Naama representing Lord Vishnu, and entertainment options like a toy train, jungle trails, a children's play park, and a gliding base. The recently introduced ropeway provides a thrilling cable car ride from the base of the hill to the park top, offering stunning aerial views.
  3. Developed by the Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region Development Authority (VMRDA), Kailasagiri is recognized as one of Andhra Pradeshâ€™s best tourist spots (awarded in 2003). It attracts around 300,000 visitors annually, both Indian and foreign. The park is maintained as a plastic-free zone to protect the environment. It is accessible by road, walking trails, and the ropeway, making it convenient for tourists.
  1. Rushikonda Beach is renowned for its pristine golden sands, clear blue waters, and picturesque surroundings including lush palm groves and rocky hillocks. It is located about 8 kilometers from Visakhapatnam and is framed by lush green hills on one side and the Bay of Bengal on the other, making it a visually stunning coastal retreat.
  2. The beach is a popular hotspot for water sports enthusiasts, offering activities such as jet skiing, windsurfing, kayaking, parasailing, snorkeling, and swimming. It caters to both beginners and experienced adventurers, with equipment rentals and trained instructors available. These activities make Rushikonda Beach a favored destination for thrill-seekers and families alike.
  3. Rushikonda Beach is well-maintained and supported by tourism facilities including beach cottages, restaurants, and the Haritha Beach Resort operated by Andhra Pradesh Tourism. It is also close to other attractions like Kailasagiri Hill Park and the Visakha Museum. The presence of the ISKCON Temple and the 14th-century Sri Sarva Rusheswara Temple nearby adds cultural significance to the visit.
